Dennis Ritchie

Here's a timeline of Dennis Ritchie's life:

  • 1941 Born in Bronxville, New York
  • 1967 Began work at Bell Labs: At the time, Bell was the premier lab in the country for electronics, and Ritchie was brought on to work in their labs.
  • 1968 Graduated Harvard University: Ritchie graduated with a bachelors degree in physics, going on to complete his Doctorate in 1968.
  • 1971 The UNIX Programmer's Manual was released: This gave the basic format for how to work with UNIX as an operating system; this was a rudimentary version of the later full program.
  • 1973 C Language was applied to UNIX: Ritchie, with the help of Kenneth Thompson, rewrote UNIX using their new language, C; it was an answer to B, which had failed them in previous application
  • 1974 First "USENIX" meeting: This collaboration of UNIX users was the first meeting of what would later form into an organization known as "USENIX"
  • 1983 Received ACM Turing Award: Both Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thompson were awarded the Turing Award for their combined work.
  • 1983 Named a Fellow by Bell Labs
  • 1983 AT&T gains ability to sell Unix as a product: The U.S. Department of Justice broke up the Bell System, getting AT&T out of the previous limitations on licensing and selling Unix; this allowed them to commercialize the system.
  • 1988 Elected to U.S. National Academy of Engineering: For his accomplishments and prolific work in the computing field, Ritchie was elected to the USNAoE.
  • 1990 Promoted at Bell Laboratories: Assigned as head of System Software Research Department
  • 1996 Release of Plan 9 and Inferno: Two new operating systems were pushed on the market in the space of two years, both developed under the direction of Ritchie at Bell Labs
  • 1998 Awarded U.S. National Medal of Technology: Ritchie, along with Kenneth Thompson, was awarded the medal for the development of UNIX.
  • 2007 Retired from Bell Labs: Ritchie stepped down as head of Bell's System Software Research Department
  • 2011 Ritchie died at the age of 70 from health complications.
